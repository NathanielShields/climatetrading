Strategy

Trade lifecycle & backtest flow

Once the signal engine fires, the rest is mechanical: pick the active CME contract, buy an ATM straddle, mark the book daily with full re-pricing, decompose every day's P&L into its physical drivers, and exit on either profit-take or signal recovery.

Daily P&L attribution

Each daily P&L is decomposed into four exact components via full re-pricing. By construction: futures + vol + theta + cross_gamma = total.

How the four components are computed

Naïve (linear) approach

Greek × shock: Δ × ΔF + ν × Δσ + Θ × Δt. Fast but lossy: leaves a non-trivial residual ("higher-order Greeks") whose interpretation depends on which Greeks you choose to keep linearised. Sum doesn't equal the true day-on-day MTM change.

What we do instead

Four two-point full re-pricings with Black-76 (the same pricer used for daily MTM):

  1. v_F: shock F only — futures effect
  2. v_σ: shock σ only — vol effect
  3. v_t: shock t only — theta effect
  4. v_1: shock all — true total

cross_gamma = v_1 − v_F − v_σ − v_t + 2v_0 is the exact remainder. Sum equals total to floating-point precision.

Execution costs

Bid-ask

Tick-based half-spread that widens with IV, widens near expiry, and grows with order size (market impact). Capped at max_spread_ticks so extreme inputs can't generate runaway costs.

Commissions

Flat per-contract fee on both legs. Default $2.50/contract.

Slippage cap

The widening factor vol_spread_sensitivity means tail-event days don't disappear into "infinite slippage" — but they do reflect real-world widening that a naive bps model would miss.

Margin & capital

Long options have no CME margin requirement beyond the premium paid. But you can still blow up by deploying too much premium at once. The MarginAccount enforces a soft cap of max_premium_pct = 25% of initial_capital. Demo run uses $5 M capital → $1.25 M max premium budget. Capital utilisation is tracked daily and surfaced in the event summary.
